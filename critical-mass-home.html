Positioning

We back the companies turning space from a frontier into industrial infrastructure — operating systems, propulsion, manufacturing, sensing, and the data layer that connects them.

The next decade of aerospace will be defined by execution, not exploration. The hardest problems are no longer reaching orbit — they are sustaining commerce there, integrating it with terrestrial systems, and translating space-based intelligence into operating advantage on Earth.

Critical Mass partners with the technical founders building that backbone. We invest with the discipline of a private fund and the technical conviction of operators who have shipped real systems.

Thesis · Why Now

A generational repricing of orbit.

Launch costs have fallen by an order of magnitude. National security demand has converged with commercial scale. And the data, autonomy, and manufacturing tools required to operate in space have matured in parallel.

For the first time, the economics of space rhyme with those of any other capital-intensive industry — durable, technical, and investable.

Launch Cost · LEO −95% Cost-per-kilogram decline since 2010 has unlocked entire categories of orbital business models.
Space Economy · 2035 $1.8T Forecast addressable market, with infrastructure and intelligence layers capturing the majority of value.
Active Satellites 10× Operational satellites in orbit have grown tenfold this decade — and the curve has not flattened.
Defense Reorientation $30B+ Annual U.S. allied spend on space-based capabilities, accelerating commercial dual-use demand.

Approach

Disciplined capital. Operating partnership.

We are not a passive checkbook. Our partners have built, operated, and capitalized companies in aerospace, defense, and technical infrastructure. We commit early, work closely, and hold long.

/ 01

Technical Conviction

We invest where we understand the physics, the supply chain, and the customer. Diligence is led by operators, not delegated to consultants.

/ 02

Concentrated Commitment

A tight portfolio of companies with meaningful ownership. We say no often so we can show up fully when we say yes.

/ 03

Long Time Horizon

Aerospace compounds slowly, then quickly. We hold for fundamentals, not market windows, and provide patient follow-on capital.

/ 04

Network as Infrastructure

Direct access to primes, agencies, allied governments, and senior operators across propulsion, sensing, and manufacturing.
